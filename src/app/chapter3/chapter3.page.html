Chapter 3 | Water

We sold my childhood home, left my childhood friends, our home church and my dad. Moving to Tennessee was one of the hardest things I've ever done. When I finally arrived- it all hit me. What I had done, who I had left— what I had left. Years of connections and relationships cultivated in a small town left to be unknown in a big city. In the middle of it all, I still didn't have a job. God had told me not to job hunt. He told me that He had a job for me and that He would come to me. So I waited. I had been here for a week, I was low on funds and desperate to get some of what I was feeling out and expressed into some form of art. My former job had paid for all my graphic design tools, it didn’t make sense to keep those subscriptions as I wouldn’t be making money that way any longer and I couldn’t afford any silk- the two ways that I knew how creativly express were exhausted. One night my mom needed to run to Walmart, I decided to come along. I ended up in the art supply aisle and I picked out a child’s watercolor set, a few brushes and cheep water color paper. I went home and started painting. I lost myself in it-when I came up for air I found more of myself in what I had made. This journey has been the undoing and the remaking. It's been shedding and becoming. I've been here for 7 months now... and the art has only continued to grow and expand. Over the last year I’ve made custom pieces for local Air bnb’s and friends apartments. I designed a series, donated the originals to a friends nonprofit fundraiser, sold some prints and taught classes on translating emotions into art. I don’t know whats yet to come. But I’m excited about it.
